Problem & Context

As artificial intelligence becomes a growing part of everyday services, the need for understandable, user-centered explanations grows as well. Most existing Explainable AI (XAI) solutions are aimed at developers, offering technical insights that do little to support everyday users in understanding or trusting the systems they interact with. In this project, conducted in collaboration with the Swedish Public Employment Services (ArbetsfÃ¶rmedlingen), we explored how XAI design principles could be applied to their AI-driven job recommendation tool, "UpptÃ¤ck andra yrken". The goal was to explore whether explainability could be used as more than just a tool for trust, to see if it could also support user understanding and improve the overall experience.

Design Process

We applied a Research through Design (RtD) methodology, utilizing our designs as a tool for research. Our process began with stakeholder interviews and reviewing previous user tests conducted by ArbetsfÃ¶rmedlingen. This gave us insights into both existing usability issues with their current design, and what technical limitations their AI had. To begin our design process, we conducted a literature review and gathered around 60 XAI-related design principles from academic research. These were then combined into five actionable XAI Core Clusters which we named:

  • Explanation & Reasoning
  • User Control
  • Context Awareness
  • Interactivity & Understandability
  • Ethics, Trust & Transparency

We used these clusters and their design principles to guide the redesign. After the redesign had been developed through Figma, pilot tests were conducted to refine the upcoming user testing process. The final user tests compared the redesign with the current design, and included think-alouds, the User Experience Questionnaire (UEQ), and semi-structured interviews.

Key Changes

The redesign introduced several changes that operationalized the XAI principles directly into the interface. Key features included:

  • Contextual Explanations â€“ Instead of a single block of text for explainability (as was used in the existing design), explanations were distributed across the flow using tooltips and contextually appropriate explanations.
  • Personalizable Input â€“ An additional step between the first job input, and the generation of the job suggestions, was added. This allowed the user to adjust what aspects of their job was relevant for the job suggestions.
  • Comparison & Overview â€“ Users could now view multiple job suggestions side-by-side, making it easier to compare them with eachother.
  • Interactive Exploration â€“ Users could now regenerate job suggestions, promoting the use of self-explanation to understand how the suggestions were generated.
  • Different ways to present the results â€“ The previous ranking feature was expanded upon, and two alternatives were added to allow users to choose how the results is presented to them. These included an interactive profession map, as well as a simple visual example.

These changes were grounded in human-centered XAI principles and aimed to support a broader range of user goals and mental models, rather than just making the AI's logic transparent.

Impact & Insights

The user tests showed clear signs of improved experience and understandability in the redesigned version. The redesign scored higher in the UEQ across the board, compared to the current design. While based on a relatively small sample, they indicated a strong positive trend. Our thematic analysis surfaced five recurring themes with the users experiences:

  • Better Understanding of system logic through contextual explanations
  • Improved Comparison between job results
  • Encouraged Self-Explanation through exploration
  • Greater Sense of Control in navigating recommendations
  • Improved flexibility in the service

The results indicate that explainability can in fact be used to support and improve both understandability, as well as the overall user experience, if applied successfully and appropriately.
